01 — Anatomy of Destruction

How knowledge dies

Across 3,700 years of recorded history, knowledge has been extinguished in remarkably consistent ways. War dominates, but religious purges and state censorship tell an equally dark story about the deliberate erasure of ideas.

When the Mongols sacked Baghdad in 1258, they threw so many books into the Tigris that a horse could cross the river walking on floating manuscripts.
— Accounts of the Fall of the House of Wisdom, 1258 AD

02 — Distribution Across Time

The rhythm of erasure

From ancient Mesopotamia to 21st-century Gaza, no era has been free of the impulse to destroy what others preserved. The 20th century stands apart; industrialized warfare made cultural obliteration faster and more total than ever before.
